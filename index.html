CFA and FINRA Exams

I am a CFA Charterholder, having passed all three exams on the first try. My work experience has required the SIE, Series 7, and Series 65 FINRA exams, so I know how complex this material can be, and how it can help you both in the job search and in the real world.

These exams are becoming more difficult than ever - the 2021 CFA Level 1 pass rate dropped to an unprecedented low of 25% from a 10-year average of 42%. While there are plenty of free and cheap self-prep resources available, the risks of under-preparing are higher than ever.

Moreover, the empathetic approach to learning through tutoring can prepare you in ways that simple topic reviews cannot, since I know firsthand the importance of morale in preparing for difficult exams. Not only will we discuss the subject material, but we will address your doubts and anxieties so that you can enter the test room with both intellectual and emotional preparation to succeed.

Resume Crafting

I have been on the hiring side of both graduate school admissions and financial services recruitment, and I can help you create or improve your resume to convincingly present your skills to both the humans and machine algorithms that will screen your job applications.

I can help with proof-reading, formatting, and arranging your resume in keeping with the styles used in financial services to most convincingly make the case for your candidacy with the recruiters who will be reviewing it.

I can also help with keyword optimization of your resume to get it past Applicant Tracking System (ATS) algorithms that automatically direct many applications to the rejection pile simply because the resume does not contain the right machine-readable words or formatting, causing undue frustration to unaware applicants.

College Admissions

I know firsthand the work that goes into maximizing your chances of admission to top universities, having applied to the most selective ones and going on to attend the University of Pennsylvania. I have subsequently participated on the admission side of the process as an alumni interviewer for UPenn.

I can help you craft or edit an application to emphasize your unique strengths as an applicant, accentuate your current accomplishments and potential future contributions to the universities you will apply to, and to proofread or help design your application essays.

Financial Modeling

I have taught financial modeling at the university graduate level, and used it in making my own business decisions. This set of skills, leveraging both basic Excel functions and process automation using Visual Basic, can be a great productivity booster that is highly sought after in the job market. I can teach you to create readable and useful models for capital budgeting, corporate financial analysis, bond pricing and risk analysis, and portfolio construction.

Check out my video on applying portfolio theory to create an optimized investment portfolio in Excel as an example of both the simplicity and sophistication of the models you can create!

Corporate Finance Topics

I have taught advanced corporate finance using real company data and case studies at the undergraduate university level, and can help you review or learn how to calculate the cost of capital, cash flows and pro-forma financial statements, net present value and other capital budgeting metrics, as well as more specialized topics like lease-versus-buy analysis, working capital management, and real options.

Not only are these skills valuable in the workplace, but they can also be applied to the budgeting and investment decisions in your own life!

Economics

Finance is built on economics, and learning the basics or details of micro- and macro-economics, as well as related issues like game theory and econometrics (statistics for economists) can help you better understand the mechanisms driving the events we observe in finance every day. Through in-depth coverage of these topics, I can help you build a foundation for success in undergraduate or graduate finance studies.
